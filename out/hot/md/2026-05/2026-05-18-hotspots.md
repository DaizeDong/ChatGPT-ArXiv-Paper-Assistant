### 5. Judge Circuits Paper Exposes Format Instability in LLM-as-a-Judge Systems  [Research]

- Category: Research
- Sources: Selected arXiv Papers

If automated evaluators change their scores based on output format rather than substance, product, research, and procurement decisions built on those evaluations can be materially distorted.

**Key takeaways:**
- "Judge Circuits" examines the increasingly important LLM-as-a-judge setup and finds that the same model can score identical content differently when the response schema changes, such as 1-5 ratings versus binary labels.
- The paper pushes beyond surface-level observations of benchmark inconsistency by analyzing the mechanism behind these formatting effects, making it more actionable for model-evaluation teams.
- The result matters for labs and enterprises using judge models in reinforcement learning, internal eval suites, and leaderboard reporting, where apparent gains may reflect prompt or format artifacts rather than genuine capability improvements.

**Sources:**
- [Judge Circuits](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.16023) (Selected arXiv Papers)
