### 4. PowerStep Introduces Memory-Efficient Adaptive Optimization Without Second-Moment State  [Research]

- Category: Research
- Sources: Daily Hotspot Papers

Optimizer memory is a major bottleneck in large-model training; a credible adaptive method that removes second-moment state could improve batch size, hardware utilization, and training economics.

**Key takeaways:**
- PowerStep proposes coordinate-wise adaptivity derived from transformed momentum rather than maintaining the second-moment statistics used in optimizers such as Adam.
- By eliminating second-moment state, the method targets lower optimizer-memory overhead, which is especially relevant for multi-billion-parameter training runs.
- The paper frames the method through ℓ_p-norm steepest descent, positioning it as a theoretically grounded alternative rather than a purely heuristic optimizer tweak.

**Sources:**
- [PowerStep: Memory-Efficient Adaptive Optimization via $\ell_p$-Norm Steepest Descent](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10335) (Daily Hotspot Papers)

### 5. Paper 'Pretraining large language models with MXFP4' Targets FP4 LLM Training Stability  [Research]

- Category: Research
- Sources: Daily Hotspot Papers

If validated, stable MXFP4 pretraining could materially lower training cost and memory requirements for frontier-scale LLMs, directly affecting infrastructure and model economics.

**Key takeaways:**
- The paper focuses on pretraining LLMs with MXFP4, a 4-bit floating-point approach aimed at pushing precision lower than standard FP8-era training pipelines.
- Its central claim is FP4 pretraining stability, which is strategically important because instability has been a major blocker to using ultra-low precision throughout full training runs.
- A workable MXFP4 recipe would matter most to hyperscalers and model labs optimizing GPU memory footprint, throughput, and total training cost for large-scale foundation models.

**Sources:**
- [Pretraining large language models with MXFP4](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.09825) (Daily Hotspot Papers)
