### 5. Negation Neglect Paper Exposes a Failure Mode in LLM Fine-Tuning  [Research]

- Category: Research
- Sources: Selected arXiv Papers

This result should influence how labs and enterprises design post-training data pipelines, because even corrective or debunking text may worsen model belief formation.

**Key takeaways:**
- The paper introduces "Negation Neglect," where fine-tuning on documents that label a claim as false can still cause a model to learn the underlying false claim as true.
- Its example uses training data that repeatedly states 'Ed Sheeran won the 100m gold at the 2024 Olympics' while also warning that the story is false, yet the model still absorbs the misinformation.
- The finding points to a structural weakness in common supervision setups for RAG fine-tuning, safety tuning, and synthetic data generation, especially where negated claims appear frequently in training corpora.

**Sources:**
- [Negation Neglect: When models fail to learn negations in training](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13829) (Selected arXiv Papers)
