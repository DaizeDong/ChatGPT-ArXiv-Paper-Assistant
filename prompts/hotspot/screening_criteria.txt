## Daily Hotspot Criteria

Evaluate each candidate topic on three axes from 1 to 10.

### QUALITY
- 9-10: strong technical, product, or research substance; clearly worth expert attention
- 7-8: meaningful and non-trivial, but narrower or less foundational
- 5-6: somewhat interesting, but limited depth or significance
- 1-4: weak, repetitive, or mostly promotional

### HEAT
- 9-10: clearly one of the day's focal points across multiple sources or communities
- 7-8: noticeably discussed or trending
- 5-6: some traction, but not a defining daily topic
- 1-4: little evidence of wider attention

### IMPORTANCE
- 9-10: likely to matter beyond the day; major release, research, platform change, or shift in direction
- 7-8: materially useful or relevant to practitioners/researchers
- 5-6: modest significance
- 1-4: low lasting impact

### Keep / Watchlist Guidance
- KEEP should be true only for the strongest topics of the day.
- WATCHLIST should be true for borderline topics that are worth tracking but not worthy of the main list.
- A topic should not be both KEEP and WATCHLIST.
- If the evidence is weak, set both KEEP and WATCHLIST to false.
- Prefer topics with independent confirmation from multiple source families.
- A single paper, single repo, or single roundup mention should usually be WATCHLIST rather than KEEP unless it is obviously one of the day's defining events.
- Do not fill the main list with several isolated paper topics from the same feed.

### ARTIFACT_TYPE
Classify the primary artifact type of this cluster:
- "paper" — an academic research paper (arXiv, conference, journal)
- "official_post" — an official blog post, announcement, or product page from a company
- "repo" — a GitHub repository, open-source tool, or code release
- "newsletter_recap" — a roundup or newsletter that summarizes other stories
- "discussion" — a forum thread, social media discussion, or community debate
- "blog_analysis" — a long-form analysis, explainer, or editorial from an independent source

### EVENT_TYPE
Classify the underlying event:
- "model_release" — a new model, checkpoint, or model update is released
- "product_launch" — a product, API, platform feature, or service is launched or updated
- "research_paper" — new research results, findings, or a method is presented
- "deep_analysis" — a substantive analysis, explainer, or technical deep-dive
- "tooling_update" — a new tool, framework, SDK, or developer resource
- "industry_move" — funding, acquisition, partnership, policy, or organizational change
- "community_discussion" — community debate, reaction, viral moment, or social buzz

### IS_RESURFACED
Set to true if the primary content is an old paper or project (older than ~30 days) that is being discussed again, not genuinely new. Trending papers on Hugging Face are often resurfaced old papers.

### Category
Choose one category:
- Research
- Product Release
- Tooling
- Industry Update
- Community Signal

### Source Tier Context
Each candidate has source tier information. Use it to calibrate trust:
- "official" tier: company blogs, official announcements — highest trust
- "trusted_research" tier: curated daily papers — high trust for research
- "trusted_analysis" tier: high-quality newsletters (The Batch, Import AI) — high trust for analysis
- "community" tier: community-curated feeds (HF trending, AINews) — medium trust
- "builder" tier: GitHub trending — medium trust for tooling
- "low_trust_recap" tier: recap newsletters (The Rundown, Superhuman) — low trust, treat as supporting evidence only
