## Daily Hotspot Criteria

Evaluate each candidate topic on three axes from 1 to 10.

### Source Tier Hierarchy (use this to calibrate your judgments)
- **Official sources** (company blogs, official announcements): highest credibility. An official_post from OpenAI News about a model release is inherently more significant than a newsletter_recap about the same topic.
- **Trusted research** (arXiv papers, HF trending with high upvotes): high credibility for research claims.
- **Trusted analysis** (expert blogs like Simon Willison, Lilian Weng, Import AI): high credibility for technical depth.
- **Community signals** (Reddit, HN, AINews, X/Twitter): valuable as corroboration, weaker as sole evidence.
- **Low-trust recaps** (The Rundown AI, Superhuman AI, The Neuron): useful for trend detection, but never sufficient as sole evidence for KEEP.

### QUALITY
- 9-10: strong technical, product, or research substance; clearly worth expert attention
- 7-8: meaningful and non-trivial, but narrower or less foundational
- 5-6: somewhat interesting, but limited depth or significance
- 1-4: weak, repetitive, or mostly promotional

### HEAT
- 9-10: clearly one of the day's focal points across multiple sources or communities
- 7-8: noticeably discussed or trending
- 5-6: some traction, but not a defining daily topic
- 1-4: little evidence of wider attention

### IMPORTANCE
- 9-10: likely to matter beyond the day; major release, research, platform change, or shift in direction
- 7-8: materially useful or relevant to practitioners/researchers
- 5-6: modest significance
- 1-4: low lasting impact

### Strict KEEP Criteria (must meet at least one)
- Official source + genuine product/model/policy announcement
- Multi-source (>=2 independent sources) convergence on the same topic
- High-impact research from trusted_research source with supporting evidence (high citations, code, benchmark results)

### WATCHLIST Criteria
- Single-source topics that are interesting but lack corroboration
- Early signals that may develop into KEEP-worthy topics

### Explicit DROP Criteria (always drop these)
- Awesome-lists and curated link collections (e.g., "awesome-llm", "awesome-agents")
- Generic agent/MCP wrapper repos with <300 stars
- Resurfaced papers (>14 days old) unless there is a specific new development
- Funding/investment news without a primary source
- Conference/workshop/hackathon announcements
- Newsletter self-promotion or subscription appeals
- Repos that are just thin wrappers around existing APIs

### Deep Reads Identification
- If a topic comes from a blog_analysis source (editorial_depth role) with substantive content, mark it with CATEGORY="Research" and note in SUMMARY that it is a deep analysis piece.
