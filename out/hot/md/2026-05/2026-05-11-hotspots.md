### 5. "Tool Calling is Linearly Readable and Steerable in Language Models" Finds Decodable Tool-Choice Signals Across 12 Models  [Research]

- Category: Research
- Sources: Selected arXiv Papers

If tool selection can be read and steered with lightweight probes, enterprises may gain a lower-cost path to improving agent reliability without retraining frontier models.

**Key takeaways:**
- The paper studies 12 instruction-tuned models spanning Gemma 3, Qwen 3, Qwen 2.5, and Llama 3.1, with parameter sizes ranging from 270M to 27B.
- Authors report that the identity of the chosen tool is linearly readable from model representations, implying tool-use decisions are encoded in a relatively accessible form before execution.
- The work also claims steerability, suggesting interventions on internal representations can shift tool choice and potentially reduce silent agent failures such as wrong API calls or missed actions.

**Sources:**
- [Tool Calling is Linearly Readable and Steerable in Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.07990) (Selected arXiv Papers)
