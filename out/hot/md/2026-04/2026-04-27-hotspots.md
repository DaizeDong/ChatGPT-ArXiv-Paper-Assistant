### 4. HGQ-LUT Paper Targets Faster LUT-Aware Training for FPGA DNN Inference  [Research]

- Category: Research
- Sources: Selected arXiv Papers

If validated, this could improve the practicality of FPGA-based AI deployment for latency- and power-sensitive edge and industrial systems.

**Key takeaways:**
- The paper, titled "HGQ-LUT: Fast LUT-Aware Training and Efficient Architectures for DNN Inference," focuses on lookup-table neural networks that map arithmetic directly onto FPGA logic primitives.
- Its core claim is to address a major adoption bottleneck in LUT-aware training, which prior methods have struggled with due to complexity and limited usability in practice.
- The work is aimed at ultra-low-latency, hardware-efficient inference, making it more relevant to edge deployment and custom accelerators than to mainstream GPU serving.

**Sources:**
- [HGQ-LUT: Fast LUT-Aware Training and Efficient Architectures for DNN Inference](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22293) (Selected arXiv Papers)

### 5. Constrained-LQR Paper Reports Near-Optimal Regret for Safe Adaptive Control  [Research]

- Category: Research
- Sources: Selected arXiv Papers

Safe learning guarantees matter for robotics, autonomy, and industrial control systems where AI policies must satisfy hard constraints during deployment, not just in simulation.

**Key takeaways:**
- The paper studies stochastic linear quadratic regulator control with constraints that must hold at every time step, a stricter setting than unconstrained adaptive control.
- It positions its result against prior multidimensional work showing roughly O(T^(2/3)) regret, framing the new contribution as a near-optimal improvement.
- The emphasis on robust constraint satisfaction makes the result potentially relevant for safety-critical applications such as autonomous systems and process control.

**Sources:**
- [Near-Optimal Regret for the Safe Learning-based Control of the Constrained Linear Quadratic Regulator](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22158) (Selected arXiv Papers)
