## Daily Hotspot Criteria

Evaluate each candidate topic on three axes from 1 to 10.

### Source Tier Hierarchy (use this to calibrate your judgments)
- **Official sources** (company blogs, official announcements): highest credibility. An official_post from OpenAI News about a model release is inherently more significant than a newsletter_recap about the same topic.
- **Trusted research** (arXiv papers, HF trending with high upvotes): high credibility for research claims.
- **Trusted analysis** (expert blogs like Simon Willison, Lilian Weng, Import AI): high credibility for technical depth.
- **Community signals** (Reddit, HN, AINews, X/Twitter): valuable as corroboration, weaker as sole evidence.
- **Low-trust recaps** (The Rundown AI, Superhuman AI, The Neuron): useful for trend detection, but never sufficient as sole evidence for KEEP.

### Artifact Requirement (MANDATORY for KEEP)
Every KEEP topic must have at least one concrete artifact:
- **Product artifact**: A named model, API, platform, SDK, or feature (e.g., "GPT-5 Turbo", "Claude 4 Tool Use API")
- **Financial artifact**: A funding event with dollar amount, valuation, or acquisition with named parties (e.g., "$500M Series C", "acquired by Google")
- **Company artifact**: A named startup launch, pivot, or major strategic move (e.g., "xAI comes out of stealth")
- **Research artifact**: A paper with verifiable SOTA claims, benchmark numbers, or novel architecture (e.g., "outperforms GPT-4o on MATH by 12%")
- **Strategic artifact**: A named partnership, regulatory change, or infrastructure investment (e.g., "NVIDIA partners with AWS for H200 deployment")

Topics WITHOUT a concrete artifact are automatically WATCHLIST or DROP, regardless of scores.

### QUALITY
- 9-10: strong technical, product, or research substance; clearly worth executive attention
- 7-8: meaningful and non-trivial, but narrower or less foundational
- 5-6: somewhat interesting, but limited depth or significance
- 1-4: weak, repetitive, or mostly promotional

### HEAT
- 9-10: clearly one of the day's focal points across multiple sources or communities
- 7-8: noticeably discussed or trending
- 5-6: some traction, but not a defining daily topic
- 1-4: little evidence of wider attention

### IMPORTANCE (calibrated for investors/executives)
- 9-10: boardroom significance — major product launch, $100M+ funding, paradigm shift, regulatory change affecting the industry
- 7-8: materially impacts investment thesis, product strategy, or research direction
- 5-6: relevant to practitioners but limited strategic impact
- 1-4: low lasting impact, would not change any decision

### Strict KEEP Criteria (must meet ALL of the following)
1. **Concrete artifact**: The topic has a specific, nameable output (see Artifact Requirement above)
2. **Frontier significance**: The topic represents a genuinely new development — a new release, a new paper, a new product, a policy shift, or a breakthrough. Discussions, commentary, or benchmarks of existing techniques do NOT qualify.
3. **Authoritative evidence**: At least one of:
   - Official source (company blog, official announcement)
   - High-impact research paper (arXiv, top venue, strong benchmarks)
   - Multi-source (>=2 independent sources) convergence
4. **Industry impact**: The topic would matter to AI investors, executives, or research leaders.

### WATCHLIST Criteria
- Single-source topics with interesting artifacts but lacking corroboration
- Early signals that may develop into KEEP-worthy topics

### Explicit DROP Criteria (always drop these)
- Community discussions, opinion threads, "what do you think" posts
- Awesome-lists and curated link collections
- Generic agent/MCP wrapper repos with <300 stars
- Resurfaced papers (>14 days old) unless there is a specific new development
- Newsletter self-promotion or subscription appeals
- Tutorials, guides, walkthroughs, cookbooks
- Personal benchmarks ("I tested X on my GPU"), hardware comparisons
- Analysis of leaked/existing source code
- Commentary or opinions without a new underlying development
- Clickbait titles ("X is dead", "mind-blowing", "you won't believe")
- Funding/investment news without a primary source or dollar amount
- Conference/workshop/hackathon announcements

### Deep Reads Identification
- If a topic comes from a blog_analysis source (editorial_depth role) with substantive content, mark it with CATEGORY="Research" and note in SUMMARY that it is a deep analysis piece.
