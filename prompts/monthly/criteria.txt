## Monthly Summary Categories

Classify each paper into exactly one primary category:

1. Model Architecture
- Core architectural ideas, routing mechanisms, attention variants, transformers, Mixture-of-Experts, autoencoders, or other foundational neural architecture contributions.

2. Model Compression and Efficiency
- Quantization, pruning, sparsity, low-rank methods, cache, inference efficiency, memory efficiency, or algorithmic efficiency improvements.

3. High Performance Computing
- Distributed training, systems innovations, throughput improvements, communication optimization, large-scale training infrastructure, or memory optimization at the systems level.

4. Representation Learning
- How models encode information, embeddings, feature learning, contrastive methods, sparse coding, dictionary learning, interpretability of internal representations, or training/generalization dynamics tied to representation learning.

5. Other Foundational Research
- Foundational or theoretical work that passes the user's research taste but does not fit cleanly into the four categories above.

## Monthly Selection Rule

These papers were already selected by the daily pipeline, but the monthly digest should still be more selective.

- KEEP_IN_MONTHLY should be true only if the paper is one of the stronger papers worth surfacing in a monthly digest.
- Be conservative: application-heavy, benchmark-heavy, survey-heavy, or only partially relevant papers should usually be excluded from the monthly digest.
- The monthly digest should be much shorter than the union of daily selections. When in doubt, exclude.
- Judge by the paper's primary contribution, not by incidental keywords.
- Prefer foundational methodological or theoretical contributions.
